Why this checklist exists

Most homeowners don't realize that the average emergency repair costs $2,400/year — and 80% of those emergencies started as $80 problems someone didn't catch in time. This is the same 12-point inspection our Gold Hands handymen run on every member visit. Walk through your home with this list once a quarter. It takes 5 minutes. It can save you a 5-figure repair.

The 12 Checks

1
Caulk around tubs, sinks, showers
Bathroom · Kitchen
Run your fingernail along every caulk seam. If it cracks, peels, or comes off — water is already getting behind the wall. Mold starts within 48 hours of moisture exposure.
If ignored: $4,000+ mold remediation + drywall If caught: $80 re-caulk job
2
HVAC air filter
Mechanical
Pull the filter. If you can't see light through it, replace it. A clogged filter forces your AC to work 30% harder, raises your power bill, and shortens unit life by 5-10 years.
If ignored: $5,000+ early HVAC failure + $200/yr extra power If caught: $20 filter
3
Water heater anode rod
Plumbing
The anode rod is a sacrificial metal that corrodes instead of your tank. After 4-5 years it's gone, and the tank starts rusting from the inside. By year 7-8 — flood. Tank replacement is the most expensive plumbing emergency.
If ignored: $1,500+ tank replacement + flood cleanup If caught: $80 anode rod swap
4
Smoke + CO detector batteries
Safety · Code
Press and hold every detector for 5 sec. Should beep loud. Most state codes require detectors within 10 ft of every bedroom. Half of home fire deaths happen in homes with non-working detectors.
If ignored: lives + $25K+ insurance issues If caught: $5/battery
5
Dryer vent lint buildup
Mechanical · Fire risk
Pull the dryer out. Disconnect the back hose. Look inside the vent — if you see lint buildup, it's a fire waiting to happen. The US Fire Administration reports 2,900 dryer fires/year. Clean once a year minimum.
If ignored: $50K+ fire damage If caught: $80 vent cleaning
6
Garage door springs
Mechanical
Look at the springs above the door. Any rust spots? Stretched coils? Springs typically last 7-10 years. They fail without warning — usually when your car is half-in. Replacing both at once is a planned $250. Replacing one in an emergency is $500+.
If ignored: $500+ emergency + car damage If caught: $250 planned replacement
7
Slow drains
Plumbing
Run every sink, shower, and tub for 60 seconds. Watch the drain. If water sits, you have a partial clog. Partial clogs become full clogs. Full clogs in main drains become $1,500 sewer-line bills.
If ignored: $1,500+ sewer line cleaning If caught: $80 snake job
8
Foundation cracks
Structural
Walk the perimeter of your home. Look at the bottom 2 ft of each wall. Hairline cracks (under 1/16") are usually cosmetic. Anything wider than a quarter — call a structural engineer. Catching settlement early saves 6-figure repairs.
If ignored: $30,000+ foundation repair If caught: $200 hairline seal
9
Window weatherstripping
Energy
Hold a candle near every window frame on a windy day. If the flame flickers, you're losing energy. Bad weatherstripping wastes 10-25% of your heating/cooling. Refresh costs $50-100 per window.
If ignored: $300-600/yr extra utility If caught: $100 weatherstrip
10
Refrigerator coils
Appliance
Pull the fridge out. Look at the coils on the back or underneath. Caked dust? That's why your kitchen is hot, your power bill is high, and your fridge will die early. Clean coils run 35% more efficiently.
If ignored: $1,200+ early fridge replacement If caught: 15 min vacuum
11
Door alignment + hinge oil
General
Open every interior door. If it doesn't close cleanly or sticks, that's settlement or wear. A drop of 3-in-1 oil on each hinge stops the squeaks. Misaligned doors over time crack their frames — $300+ to rebuild.
If ignored: $300+ frame rebuild per door If caught: $5 oil + 10 min adjustment
12
GFCI outlets in bath/kitchen
Electrical · Code
In every bathroom, kitchen, garage, and outdoor outlet — press the «test» button. Should pop out. Press «reset» — should pop back. If either fails, the outlet is dead and a real shock hazard. Required by code in every state.
If ignored: shock hazard + insurance void If caught: $40 GFCI outlet
