The Real Story

Why "easy" TV mounts go wrong — and what we do differently.

TV mounting and furniture assembly look simple on a Saturday afternoon YouTube video. The reality is different. Drop a 65-inch OLED off the wall once and you've turned a $30 bracket project into a $1,800 mistake — plus drywall repair, plus your spouse's expression. We handle the boring parts that make the visible parts work.

The DIY pitfalls we see every week

Most weekend installs we get called to fix have the same handful of mistakes. They aren't exotic — they're the predictable failure modes when somebody trusts a packaging diagram instead of a stud finder.

  • Wall anchors that pull out under 50 lb load. Plastic butterfly anchors are rated optimistically. A 55-inch TV plus a tilt mount plus a curious toddler equals 60+ lb of dynamic load. Anchors fail; drywall craters. We always anchor into studs, period.
  • Off-center mounts because the studs weren't where the homeowner wanted the TV. Studs are spaced 16 inches on center in most US homes. If your couch is centered between two studs, an amateur installer puts the TV 8 inches off-axis. We use a properly-rated articulating arm or sliding bracket to recenter.
  • Cables draped down the wall. Code-compliant in-wall HDMI runs require CL2 or CL3 rated cable. Romex extension power cords are not code-compliant inside walls — they're a fire-code violation. We use a recessed power kit (rough-in box + power flex) which is the only legal way to do this.
  • IKEA dressers without anti-tip straps. The MALM line has been the subject of a 17.3 million unit recall. Every chest of drawers we assemble for a household with kids gets wall-strapped — it's a 10-minute step that prevents tragedy.
  • Tile-mounted TVs without diamond bits. Standard masonry bits crack porcelain. We use diamond-tipped bits with a water-feed sponge and start every hole at low RPM with center-punch tape to stop the bit from walking.

Tools and skills you'd otherwise need to buy

If you wanted to do a single TV install correctly, here's the kit: a quality stud finder (Franklin ProSensor, $50), a torpedo level, a 4-foot bubble level, a hammer drill, a diamond bit set if tile is involved, masonry sleeve anchors, in-wall cable management kit, recessed power kit, and a magnetic wristband for hardware. About $250 in tools you use once. Plus 90 minutes of YouTube to learn what you're looking at on a stud finder display. Or we can come over.

Safety considerations we won't shortcut

Anchoring into a stud requires confirming what's actually there. We probe with a thin trim nail before the lag bolt goes in — a 3-second check that catches the rare situation where an old plumbing line or BX cable is running through what looks like a stud. We also confirm the mount's weight rating against the actual TV weight (not the box weight) and we install with a 2× safety margin on hardware. For tilting mounts, we set the tilt with the TV connected to verify it doesn't fall out of the lock notches under its own weight.

FAQ

TV Mounting & Assembly Questions

Can you mount a TV on a brick or concrete wall?

Yes. Brick and poured concrete are actually stronger anchor surfaces than drywall — we use masonry sleeve anchors rated for the TV's weight class. Hollow concrete block needs toggle anchors instead. We bring both kits standard so we can finish the job in one visit.

Will you hide the cables behind the wall?

Yes — we run a low-voltage in-wall cable kit (rated CL2/CL3) for HDMI and a recessed power kit for the outlet behind the TV. This is fully code-compliant. Standard romex cannot be re-routed inside walls without a licensed electrical permit, so we use the proper recessed power solution.

What's the largest TV you'll mount?

We routinely mount up to 85 inches. Anything 75 inches and above we anchor into a minimum of two studs with a heavy-duty articulating or fixed mount rated for the TV's weight plus a 2× safety margin. We require the homeowner to supply the mount unless we're picking it up as a paid extra.

Can you assemble IKEA and Wayfair furniture?

Yes — IKEA, Wayfair, West Elm, Article, Pottery Barn, Crate & Barrel, all of it. We've assembled thousands of pieces and we know the gotchas: PAX wardrobes that need wall-anchoring, MALM dressers that ship with the wrong cam locks, KALLAX shelves that warp without anti-tip straps. We bring our own bit set, levels, and clamps.

Do you remove and dispose of the old TV?

Yes, for an additional fee. CRT TVs require certified e-waste disposal in most states — we handle that. Flat-screens we can either re-mount in another room as part of the same visit or take to a local recycler.

Is TV mounting included in the membership?

Members get a 10–20% discount on labor depending on tier (Bronze 10%, Silver 15%, Gold 20%). For Silver and Gold members, a single TV mount or a small piece of furniture assembly typically fits inside your monthly visit at no extra labor charge.
